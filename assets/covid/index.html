Community Cases

Community cases are measured as declared by the Government of Singapore - cases in the un-isolated community in Singapore

These do not include imported cases, as well as cases in isolated dormitories

Graphs explained

The top two graphs simply indicate the total cases and new cases per day in Singapore

Hovering over or interacting with the graphs allows you to see only community cases, or total cases

The bottom two graphs indicate the new cases on a day, compared to the total cases till that day, which can be used to measure the exponential nature of the virus

Having the those graphs be in the shape of a straight line indicates exponential growth, which means being below the line indicates the curve being flattened

The straight lines used are ones that were fitted at the beggining of the pandemic in Singapore, and hence show the improvements since then
