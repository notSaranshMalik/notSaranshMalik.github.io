More Information

This single pendulum system was programmed using JavaScript. It uses real values to simulate it, by including regular gravitational force as well as air resistance.

This uses a numerical approach by sampling values 60 times per second and updating the graph in real time.

Here are the formulae used:

Gravitational angular acceleration = field strength × sin(angle) / pendulum length

α = g × sin(a) / l

Here is the derivation:

α = τ × sin(a) / I

α = m × g × l × sin(a) / m × l2

α = g × sin(a) / l

Air resistance angular acceleration = 0.375 × drag coefficient × air density × velocity2 / (bob density × bob radius × pendulum length

α = 0.375 × C × ρair × v2/ (ρbob × r × l)

Here is the derivation:

F = 0.5 × ρair × A × C × v2

m × a = 0.5 × ρair × π × r2 × C × v2

α × l = 0.5 × ρair × π × r2 × C × v2 / m

α = 0.5 × ρair × π × r2 × C × v2 / ((4/3) × π × r3 × ρbob × l)

α = 0.375 × C × ρair × v2 / (ρbob × r × l)
